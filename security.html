Tree Rings

Tree ring dating. Longer it takes to crack your password, thicker your trunk. Password-cracking times were calculated by finding the average of MD5, SHA1, MD5Crypt and Bcrypt password-cracking rates, with calculated rate information from oclhashcat.

Information

Novelty: Not many password security meters use a responsive graphical interface to display the amount of time it would take to crack a password.

Design: It's aesthetically pleasing, consistent, prevents errors as much as possible, and is intuitive and easy to use, among other things.

Reusable: These three widgets can be reused on other websites -- Dendrochronoly would be made smaller. Pressing the "Reusable?" button at the top will convert the Traffic and Bricks widgets to fit tighter spaces to demonstrate their flexibility.

All password strengths were calculated using the same algorithm, which takes into account password length and the inclusion of different characters and finds the number of seconds it would take for different computers to crack it, returning all the different rates' averages. Then it translates seconds into legible time (minutes, hours, days, years).

For Dendrochonology, I create a new canvas with a circle every time the password-cracking time changes to reflect the addition of a new tree ring. I also control for the edge case of window resizing.
